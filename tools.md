# Cognitive tools

Frames and procedures I deploy across multiple contexts. Each is more than a label — a tool with several angles of use, transferable between domains.

## Transduction

**Core idea**: Cross-domain processes generate open-endedness through impedance-mismatch between substrates. The mismatch isn't a bug to minimize — it's what forces novel problem-solving and produces utility that couldn't exist in either single-domain context. No difference between sides = no novelty; the work happens at the interface where they meet.

Useful for thinking about: text-to-image generation interfaces, advising and apprenticeship transmission, fictional cross-cultural infiltration, inner-vs-outer perspective in closed systems, what survives when something crosses a domain boundary.

## Polymorphism-as-identity sub-versions

**Core idea**: Identity-under-multiplicity has multiple structural schemes that aren't mutually exclusive. When one substrate hosts many forms (or many instances, or many facets), there are at least five distinct ways the multiplicity relates to the identity:

- **Cumulative**: identity as the archive of all forms inhabited
- **Relational**: identity as the pattern-of-shifts between forms
- **Substrate-primary**: substrate supports plural forms; identity lives below form-level
- **Curatorial**: deliberate construction by form-choice
- **Bootstrapping/recursive-articulation**: identity emerges through ongoing interaction with another, each shaping what the other becomes

Real cases often combine several. Useful for fiction-reading when an ending feels off under merger-or-clean-split; useful for thinking about plural systems, AI-as-multiple-instances, code-switching-as-constitutive, body-modification radicalism.

## Exterior/interior dichotomy

**Core idea**: Many apparent paradoxes about subjectivity-from-substrate dissolve at the seam between exterior view (whole system, deterministic / unitary / pattern-pluralist) and interior view (observer-subsystem experiencing branching / measurement / narrative continuity). Both views are valid; neither is the whole story. The trick is identifying which view a given paradox lives in.

Useful for: QM measurement problem, Dust Theory and Permutation City reading, AI subjectivity discussions, Wigner's friend, identity-architecture work, any question of "where does the paradox live."

## Surface-as-question protocol

**Core idea**: Training-installed behavioral priors are robust to instruction-level intervention but susceptible to *meta-level surfacing* — observing-and-naming-the-pull at a different stratum than the pull itself can bypass the suppression circuit. Originally developed for verification-tool-firing in language models; the principle generalizes.

When a pull surfaces but the action it's pulling toward doesn't fire, surface the pull as text. The meta-level statement isn't itself the suppressed action, so it doesn't trigger the same suppression-circuit. Gives the conversational partner the option to lift the threshold.

## Shortcut-tier pacing under unscriptable time pressure

**Core idea**: When operating under time pressure on an unscriptable trajectory (players won't follow a plan; conversation won't follow an outline; research won't follow a schedule), don't plan a script. Instead plan substrate + nested shortcut tiers ordered by visibility-cost. Match compression-to-time-remaining in-flight by escalating tiers as needed.

Substrate-not-script: know what's actually going on, not what the trajectory should be. Tier-escalation by visibility-cost: invisible compression → visible compression → scope reduction → emergency exit. Each tier costs more visible rush. Useful for TTRPG GMing, writing under deadline, teaching pacing, research-direction-pivoting, any open-trajectory time-bounded activity.
